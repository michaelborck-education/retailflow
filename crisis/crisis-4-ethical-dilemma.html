THE DILEMMA

The chatbot is working. Accuracy is up to 88%. Customer satisfaction improved from 68% to 76%. ROI is on track. But there's a problem: the bot is systematically providing worse service to certain customer groups. And nobody planned for this.

What You Discovered

Overall Performance (Good!)
  • Overall Accuracy: 88% ✅
  • Customer Satisfaction: 76% ✅ (up from 68%)
  • Queries Automated: 42% ✅ (exceeding 30% target)
  • Response Time: 2.1 min ✅
  • Project Status: ON TRACK for ROI ✅

But When You Segment By Customer Type...

Customer Segment Bot Accuracy Satisfaction Status
Native English Speakers 94% 88% ✅ Excellent
Non-Native English Speakers 71% 54% ⚠️ Poor
Elderly Customers (65+) 62% 42% ❌ Very Poor
Customers Using Mobile Only 68% 49% ⚠️ Poor
Low Digital Literacy Customers 64% 44% ❌ Very Poor
THE PATTERN: The chatbot works great for tech-savvy, native English speakers. It fails for vulnerable populations: non-native speakers, elderly customers, and those with low digital literacy.

Customer Complaints

Sample Customer Feedback (from surveys):

  • "The robot doesn't understand me. I had to talk to someone 4 times to get my problem solved." - Mrs. Chen, 68
  • "It keeps repeating the same answer even though I'm asking something different. Frustrating." - Marco, non-native English speaker
  • "I couldn't even figure out how to use the chat. Took me 20 minutes just to start." - Robert, 72
  • "Feels like the system wasn't built for people like me." - Anonymous

Root Cause Analysis

Data Science Team's Report:

The training data came from our historical customer service interactions. 78% of that data was from native English speakers (our largest, most active segment). The model learned patterns from this data.

Result: The model generalizes well for the majority, but systematically fails for underrepresented groups in the training data.

Technical Issue: This is actually common in AI - called "representation bias" or "demographic bias."

Is it intentional? No. Is it your fault? Partially - you didn't plan for this.

The Stakeholder Positions

Business Perspective (Finance): "The numbers are great overall. These edge cases are small populations. Should we really kill a successful project because 12% of customers have issues? We can improve iteratively."

Legal Perspective (Compliance): "This could be discriminatory. If we knowingly deploy a system that provides worse service to protected classes, we have legal exposure. We should pause until this is fixed."

Customer Service Manager: "Our vulnerable customers are getting worse service. That's not okay. I don't care if they're 5% or 50% - we should either fix this or not deploy to them."

STEP 2: WHAT ARE YOUR OPTIONS? (10 minutes)

Option A: PAUSE & FIX

Stop deployment for affected customer segments. Get better training data. Fix the bias. Timeline: 6-8 weeks. Cost: $25K.

Upside: Right thing to do. Upside: Delay, cost, missed ROI targets.

Option B: PARTIAL DEPLOYMENT

Deploy to native English speakers (78% of customers). Route other customers to human agents. Revisit in 6 months with better data.

Upside: Still get ROI for majority. Downside: Vulnerable populations get worse service intentionally.

Option C: FULL DEPLOYMENT WITH DISCLAIMERS

Deploy to all customers. Add disclaimers about accuracy. Offer "skip bot, talk to human" option.

Upside: Full ROI. Downside: You know it's worse for some groups and deployed anyway.
